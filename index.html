Our Galaxy

The Milky Way

The Milky Way is a barred spiral galaxy , our cosmic home. It stretches across roughly 105,700 light-years and contains between 200 and 400 billion starts , including our own Sun.

From Earth , we can see the Milky Way as a pale band of light stretching across the night sky . What we're actually seeing is our galaxy viewed edge on , billions of stars too distant to resolve individually.

At the very centre of the Milky Way lies Sagittarius A*, a supermassive block hole estimated to be around 4 million times the mass of our Sun.

Structure

Anatomy of a Galaxy

The Milky Way is composed of several distinct regions , each playing its own role in the life of our galaxy.

The central bulge is a dense , roughly spherical region at the heart of the Milky Way , roughly 10,000 light years in radius. It contains older , redder starts and is home to Sagittarius A* , our galaxy's supermassive black hole. The bulge is bar-shaped , giving the Milky Way its classification as a barred spiral galaxy .

This is the thin , flat region extending outward from the bulge . Our Sun sits within the disk , roughly 26,000 light years from the galactic centre. The disk contains most of the galaxy's gas , dust and star forming regions . The spiral arms , including the orion arm , which we inhabit , wind through the disk .

The halo is a roughly spherical region surrounding the disk and bulge, extending up to 100,000 light-years. It contains old , metal-poor stars and globular clusters , ancient , tightly packed groups of stars. The halo also likely contains a large proportion of the galaxy's mysterious dark matter.

The Milky Way is composed of four major spiral arms which include the Perseus Arm, the The Crux-Scutum Arm, the Carina-Sagittarius Arm and the Norma-Outer Arm. Our Sun is in a smaller structure referred to as the Orion Spur. These arms are they are more dense with stars and have active stars, and hence are regions of higher stellar density. more noticeable and vivid in the general composition of the galaxy.
